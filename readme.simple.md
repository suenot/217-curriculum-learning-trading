# Curriculum Learning for Trading -- Explained Simply

Imagine learning math starting with addition before multiplication -- easier stuff first, then harder stuff once you've got the basics down. That's exactly what curriculum learning does for trading robots!

## What is it?

When we teach a computer to trade, we don't throw it into the craziest markets right away. Instead, we start with calm, easy markets where prices move smoothly. Once the robot gets good at those, we move to trickier markets. And finally, we let it practice on the wildest, most chaotic markets.

It's like learning to swim: first the kiddie pool, then the regular pool, then the ocean waves!

## How does it work?

1. **Sort the markets**: We look at all past market days and sort them from "calm and easy" to "wild and crazy"
2. **Start easy**: The robot practices trading on the calm days first
3. **Level up**: Once it's doing well, we give it medium-difficulty days
4. **Boss level**: Finally, it faces the hardest markets -- crashes, sudden drops, and wild swings

## Why does it matter?

- The robot learns faster because it's not confused by crazy stuff at the beginning
- It builds good habits on easy markets before facing hard ones
- It's less likely to panic and lose money when things get tough, because it learned step by step

## A real example

Think about learning to ride a bike:
- First, you use training wheels (easy markets)
- Then you ride on a smooth, flat road (medium markets)
- Finally, you can handle hills and bumpy trails (hard markets)

If someone put you on a mountain trail on day one, you'd crash! But by building up slowly, you become a great rider.

That's curriculum learning -- teaching trading robots the smart way, one step at a time!
